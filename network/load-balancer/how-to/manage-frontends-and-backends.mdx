When you [create your Load Balancer](/network/load-balancer/how-to/create-load-balancer/), you set up one [frontend](/network/load-balancer/concepts/#frontends) and one [backend](/network/load-balancer/concepts/#backends) (which is attached to one or several backend servers). This page shows you how you can add additional frontends and backends to your Load Balancer, for example to balance several different applications by creating frontends listening on different ports each of which forwards traffic to its own backend or set of backends.

<Macro id="iam-requirements" />

<Message type="requirement">
  - You have an account and are logged into the [Scaleway console](https://console.scaleway.com)
  - You have [created a Load Balancer](/network/load-balancer/how-to/create-load-balancer)
</Message>

## How to add frontends

1. Click **Load Balancers** in the **Network** section of the [Scaleway Console](https://console.scaleway.com) side menu. The Load Balancer dashboard displays.
2. Click the Load Balancer you want to add frontends to. The Load Balancer information page displays.
3. Click the **Frontends** tab.
4. Click the <Icon name="plus" /> button.
    <Lightbox src="scaleway-add-frontends.webp" />
5. Enter a **name**, **port** and (if necessary) [**SSL certificate(s)**](/network/load-balancer/how-to/add-certificate) for your new frontend. Note that each frontend must listen on a different port. For help configuring your frontend, see our [dedicated reference documentation](/network/load-balancer/reference-content/configuring-load-balancer).
6. Choose between **using an existing backend** or **creating a new backend**.
    - If using an existing backend, click **Use an existing Backend** and select the backend of your choice from the dropdown list.
    - If creating a new backend, click **Create a new Backend** This will open a new tab in your browser for you to configure your backend. Enter the required information and advanced settings as required. For help configuring your backend, see our [dedicated reference documentation](/network/load-balancer/reference-content/configuring-load-balancer)
7. Under **Advanced Settings**, optionally use the <Icon name="toggle" /> to enable HTTP/3. The availability of this setting depends on your [Load Balancer's configuration](/network/load-balancer/reference-content/http2-http3/)
8. Click **Create Frontend**.
  <Message type="note">
    * By default, each Load Balancer is created using [round-robin](/network/load-balancer/concepts/#balancing-rules) balancing rules. To modify the balancing algorithm, you must use the `forward-port-algorithm` parameter in the Scaleway [API](https://www.scaleway.com/en/developers/api/load-balancer/zoned-api/), [CLI](https://github.com/scaleway/scaleway-cli/blob/master/docs/commands/lb.md) or [Terraform](https://registry.terraform.io/providers/scaleway/scaleway/latest/docs/resources/lb).
  </Message>

## How to edit frontends

1. Click **Load Balancers** in the **Network** section of the [Scaleway Console](https://console.scaleway.com) side menu. The Load Balancer dashboard displays.
2. Click the Load Balancer you want to edit. The Load Balancer information page displays.
3. Click the **Frontends** tab.
4. Click <Icon name="more" /> next to the frontend you want to edit
5. Click **Edit**.
6. Make your edits. You can change:
    - Frontend name
    - Port
    - SSL Certificate(s)
    - Attached backend
    - [HTTP/3 activation](/network/load-balancer/reference-content/http2-http3/#http3) (under **Advanced Settings**)
4. Click **Edit Frontend**.

## How to add backends

1. Click **Load Balancers** in the **Network** section of the [Scaleway Console](https://console.scaleway.com) side menu. The Load Balancer dashboard displays.
2. Click the Load Balancer you want to add backends to. The Load Balancer information page displays.
3. Click the **Backends** tab.
4. Click the <Icon name="plus" /> button.
5. Enter a name, protocol, port, TLS encryption setting, proxy, health check type,  server IP(s) and advanced settings as required for your new backend. For help configuring your backend, see our [dedicated reference documentation](/network/load-balancer/reference-content/configuring-load-balancer)
6. Click **Create Backend**.

## How to edit backends

1. Click **Load Balancers** in the **Network** section of the [Scaleway Console](https://console.scaleway.com) side menu. The Load Balancer dashboard displays.
2. Click the Load Balancer you want to edit. The Load Balancer information page displays.
3. Click the **Backends** tab.
4. Click the <Icon name="edit" /> icon next to the backend you want to edit.
5. Make your edits. 
    <Lightbox src="scaleway-edit-backends.webp" />
6. Click **Edit backend** once your edits are done.
