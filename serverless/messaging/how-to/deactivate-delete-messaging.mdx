This page shows how to deactivate an SQS or SNS service, or delete a NATS account. These two processes are different, but equivalent.

<Macro id="iam-requirements" />

<Message type="requirement">
  - You have an account and are logged into the [Scaleway console](https://console.scaleway.com)
  - You have [activated SQS or SNS, or created a NATS account](/serverless/messaging/how-to/get-started/)
</Message>

## How to deactivate SNS or SQS

<Message type="note">
You must first delete all associated queues/topics and credentials in order to deactivate SQS or SNS.
</Message>

1. Click **Messaging** in the **Serverless** section of the side menu. The list of Messaging and Queuing protocols displays.

2. Click **SQS** or **SNS** depending on which protocol you want to deactivate.

3. Click the **Settings** tab.

4. In the bottom panel, click the **Deactivate SQS** button.

    A popup displays, reminding you that you will not be able to create any new queues/topics or credentials unless you reactivate the protocol.

5. Click **Deactivate SQS** (or SNS) to confirm.

## How to delete a NATS account

1. Click **Messaging** in the **Serverless** section of the side menu. The list of Messaging and Queuing protocols displays.

2. Click **NATS**, then click the NATS account you want to delete.

3. In the bottom panel, click the **Delete NATS account** button.

    A popup displays, reminding you that the action is irreversible, and any associated queued messages and credentials will be deleted.

5. Type `DELETE` and click **Delete NATS account** to confirm.
