You can leverage our IPFS pinning service to store permanently your data on the public IPFS network. You can do it via a Content-ID (CID) or an URL. You can [create](/storage/ipfs/api-cli/volumes-operations) and manage your volume  via the [Kubo client](https://docs.ipfs.tech/install/command-line/) and the [IPFS Desktop](https://docs.ipfs.tech/install/ipfs-desktop/).

## Requirements to use IPFS Desktop

<Macro id="iam-requirements" />

<Message type="requirement">
    - You have an account and are logged into the [Scaleway Console](https://console.scaleway.com/)
    - You have generated your [API key](/identity-and-access-management/iam/how-to/create-api-keys/)
    - You have downloaded and installed the [IPFS Desktop](https://docs.ipfs.tech/install/ipfs-desktop/)
    - You have create a [Volume](/storage/ipfs/api-cli/volumes-operations/)
</Message>

## Add Scaleway as a remote pinning service

This operation allows you to add Scaleway as a remote service in your IPFS desktop.

1. Open IPFS Desktop
2. Go to **Settings**
3. Click on **Add Service**
4. Click **Add a custom one**
5. Fill in the different fields:
    - Nickname: `Scaleway`
    - API Endpoint: `https://fr-par.ipfs.labs.scaleway.com`
    - Secret access token: `<SECRET_KEY>`

<Message type="note">
    As our service is available in several regions, you can use its different endpoints:
    - `https://pl-waw.ipfs.labs.scw.cloud`
    - `https://nl-ams.ipfs.labs.scw.cloud`
    - `https://fr-par.ipfs.labs.scw.cloud`
</Message>

## Add content from the IPFS Desktop

1. Open IPFS Desktop.
2. Go to **Files**.
3. Click **import**.
4. Click **From IPFS**.
5. Enter the CID of your content.
6. Click **...** on the right.
7. Click **Set Pinning** to your Scaleway remote service.

<Lightbox src="set-pinning-service.webp" alt="" />

<Message type="note">
    You can click on Files instead of From IPFS to upload a file directly to your IPFS node.
</Message>
