Scaleway VPC Peering natively supports transitive peering.

Transitive peering (or transitive routing) means that two VPCs that are not directly peered can still exchange traffic through a third network that is peered with both of them. In other words, the routing information passes through the intermediate network, making the peering relationship transitive.

The **Hub and spoke** network topology is typical architecture leveraging transitive peering. Each node is connected to a central server, and can communicate with other nodes via this hub.
