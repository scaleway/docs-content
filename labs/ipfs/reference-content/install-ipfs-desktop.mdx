With Scaleway IPFS pinning, you can permanently store your data on the public IPFS network. You can either [create](/labs/ipfs/api-cli/volumes-operations) and manage your volume  via the [Kubo client](https://docs.ipfs.tech/install/command-line/) or through [IPFS Desktop](https://docs.ipfs.tech/install/ipfs-desktop/). 

This article shows you how to add Scaleway as your remote pinning service using IPFS Desktop.

<Macro id="iam-requirements" />

<Message type="requirement">
    - You have an account and are logged into the [Scaleway console](https://console.scaleway.com/)
    - You have generated your [API key](/identity-and-access-management/iam/how-to/create-api-keys/)
    - You have downloaded and installed [IPFS Desktop](https://docs.ipfs.tech/install/ipfs-desktop/)
    - You have created a [volume](/labs/ipfs/how-to/create-volume/)
</Message>

## Retrieve the ID of your volume

1. Click **IPFS Pinning** in the **Labs** section of the [Scaleway console](https://console.scaleway.com) side menu. The IPFS Pinning section displays.
2. Click the <Icon name="seeMore" /> icon next to your desired volume. The **Pins** section displays.
3. Click the **Settings** tab next to **Pins**. The **Settings** section displays.
4. Retrieve the ID of your volume. You will need it for the next procedure.


## Add Scaleway as a remote pinning service

This operation allows you to add Scaleway as a remote service in your IPFS desktop.

1. Open IPFS Desktop.
2. Click **Settings** on the sidebar menu. The settings panel displays.
3. Click the **Add Service** button on the **Pinning services** section of the panel. A pop-up displays asking you to select a pinning service provider.
4. Click **Add a custom one** to add Scaleway as a remote pinning service. A configuration form displays.
5. Fill in the different fields with the following information:
    - Nickname: `Scaleway`
    - API endpoint: `https:/<your_volume_region_ID>.ipfs.labs.scw.cloud/<your_volume_ID>`
    - Secret access token: `<$SCW_SECRET_KEY>`
  <Message type="important">
    Don't forget to replace the parameters in brackets with the relevant information.
  </Message>

  <Message type="note">
    Different endpoints are available depending on the regions you created your volume in.
    - `https://fr-par.ipfs.labs.scw.cloud` for volumes created in the `fr-par`region.
    - `https://nl-ams.ipfs.labs.scw.cloud` for volumes created in the `nl-ams` region.
    - `https://pl-waw.ipfs.labs.scw.cloud` for volumes created in the `pl-waw` region.
  </Message>

Scaleway should now appear in the list of remote pinning services. 

## Add content to IPFS Desktop

IPFS supports a wide range of file types, allowing users to store and share diverse content such as text, images, audio, videos or any other file type. You can also import entire directories, making IPFS a versatile tool for decentralized data storage and sharing.

1. Click **Files** on the sidebar menu. The files panel displays.
2. Click the **Import** button at the top right corner of your screen. A pop-up displays with various importation options.
3. Import the content of your choice. Your content appears in your list of files.

## Add content from the IPFS Desktop

1. Click **Files** on the sidebar menu. The files panel displays a list of your content.
2. Click the `...` button next to the content of your choice, content of your choice. A pop-up with various options displays.
3. Click `Set pinning`. A pop-up appears asking you to select where you would like to pin your content.
4. Tick `Scaleway` to select us as your remote pinning service.
2. Click **Apply** to confirm your choice. 

Your content has been transferred to the Scaleway console. You can refer to our documentation to find out [how to retrieve your content](/en/docs/labs/ipfs/how-to/add-a-pin/#how-to-retrieve-your-data) using the Scaleway console.
